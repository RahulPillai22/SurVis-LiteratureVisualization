TechWordNet: Development of semantic relation for technology information analysis using F-term and natural language processing

Abstract

Text analysis on technology has recently been progressing from the level of words to semantic relations between words. However, existing research methods, such as Subject-Action-Object, have focused on specific purposes or analytical techniques. There is an insufficient amount of fundamental study on what types of semantic relations in technical information need to be analysed to provide meaningful information. At the same time, in the field of NLP, the deep learning-based semantic relation model has been establishing as useful for specific tasks. However, there is a limit to applying the NLP model itself for technical analysis because it does not consider the characteristics of the textual information about technology. Therefore, this study proposes a deep learning-based semantic relation model for technology information analysis. First, meaningful types of semantic relations are derived from the text information about technology. By analysing the F-term classification code, which is a multi-dimensional technology hierarchy with descriptions, a technology semantic labelled dataset is constructed. Finally, we develop a classification model that analyses the semantic relations of technology based on the sentence embedding model. This study contributes to the construction of a deep learning model by developing a meaningful type in the analysis of technical information and constructing a technical text dataset with labels. The result of semantic technology relations can also be utilized as a high-quality source for various applications on technology analysis, such as technology tree and technology roadmap. In other words, it has the advantage of being able to provide generalizable technical information that is not dependent on a specific analysis purpose.

Introduction

Recently, as extensive technology-related textual data have been accumulated, technology intelligence using various text mining approaches has been intensively studied. In particular, the patent database contains textual information written and qualitatively verified by experts, so technology planning studies that analyse patent data are actively being conducted. In the early stage, the study was performed using keyword analysis as the simplest approach, frequency of words, imparting of technology trends, or exploration of technologic opportunities (Yoon & Park, 2004,2005). Extending beyond the keyword level, various studies have attempted research at the topic level by using a probabilistic model between words and documents (Chen, Zhang, Zhu, & Lu, 2017; Choi & Song, 2018; Park, Jeong, Yoon, & Mortara, 2015). Most recently, the big data approach has attracted substantial attention. Through natural language processing (NLP), more detailed grammatical and syntactic information can be analysed to provide sophisticated results for technology-related content analysis. The word embedding models have enabled similarity analysis, and they have been applied to patent infringement (Kim & Yoon, 2021), technology opportunity discovery, and other areas (He, Meng, Wu, Chan, & Pang, 2020; Kim, Park, & Yoon, 2020; Lee, Jeon, Ahn, & Kwon, 2020).

Research on semantic relations of technology perspectives is being actively conducted using systematic approaches to provide in-depth meaning and enable further applications. Technology-related information is often expressed in multi-words representing hierarchical features (Yang & Soo, 2012). In addition, syntactic relations of a clause reflect certain semantic features, such as technical problems and solutions. Because of these lexical and syntactic traits in technology information, it is imperative to analyse semantic relations. Subject-Action-Object (SAO) analysis is one of the representative approaches to investigating solutions to technical problems based on semantic relations (Wang et al., 2017). One study systematically constructed a technology tree by analysing SAO relations, where AO relations are categorized into partitive, effect, and attribute types by defining the list of verbs (Choi, Park, Kang, Lee, & Kim, 2012). The semantic relation of the technology based on the SAO structure was used to calculate the similarity between patents (Wang et al., 2019); it was then applied to tasks related to technology management, such as drawing outliers (Yoon & Kim, 2012), analysing technology trends (Yoon & Kim, 2011), and identifying technological competition trends (Yoon, Park, & Kim, 2013) through the similarity between the SAO configurations of patents. Unlike SAO, which focuses on the syntactic relationships between nouns and verbs, a preposition-based semantic analysis was suggested to identify technological relationships (inclusion, objective, effect, process, and likeness) based on keyword network structures (An, Kim, Mortara, & Lee, 2018).

Technology performs functions with complex relations in the form of systems (Hughes, 1987). Due to the characteristics of these technologies, traditional studies have conceptually presented relational structures from the point of view of technological systems (Van Wyk, 2004, Van Wyk, 2010). However, in the study reviewed above, the relationship between technologies from the point of view of the technology system was not reflected in their analysis of the relationship information of technology. They have ascertained systematic frameworks that can be analysed using NLP and other text mining techniques. Studies that investigated the semantic relations based on SAO (Choi et al., 2012) and prepositions (An et al., 2018) did not provide abundant rationales for their definitions of the relation types. Depending on how the type of semantic relationship is defined, its usefulness may be expanded when used in actual fields, which requires supplementation. In addition, previous approaches, both the SAO and preposition-based frameworks, only provide results in a limited range in accordance with the predefined lexical and syntactic patterns (Kim et al., 2020). In other words, the SAO structure cannot analyse text information expressed by verbs that have not been defined in advance. Further studies are still needed to elucidate how big technology data can be systematically analysed. In summary, in existing research, the relationship to be analysed was derived by limiting the relationship to one suitable for a specific purpose, such as a technology tree (Choi et al., 2012) or technology roadmap (Wang et al., 2015), or by focusing on the relationship that can be analysed using techniques such as text processing. In other words, a research framework was presented that depends on the analysis method rather than the consideration of the analysis target.

Most tasks in natural language processing have relied on rule-based approaches designed by language experts. The rule-based framework guarantees accuracy with respect to a set rule, but it has limitations in that inference is impossible and the cost is very high. With the continued development of deep learning models that guarantee high performance and high speed, various learning models have also been used in the field of natural language processing. In particular, among the tasks of natural language processing, research is actively being conducted in the field of semantic relation extraction and classification by using deep learning models, as these have the advantage of being classification models that can secure relatively clear correct answer data. Attempts to apply natural language processing and deep learning to tasks on technology intelligence have progressed as well (Kim et al., 2020; Krestel, Chikkamath, Hewel, & Risch, 2021). Specifically, the patent database represented by technical information has been the most utilized. In the field of natural language processing of technical texts, many studies have conducted the embedding of unstructured texts for representation. However, the embedding model for technical words and documents cannot interpret features that can be used to elucidate the relationship between technical elements. However, since the task in the existing NLP, which is learned from classical literature or news articles, does not consider the characteristics of technology information, it is not appropriate to use the previously developed model as is. To develop a technology-related semantic relation model based on a deep learning model, the following factors must be considered: First, the semantic relations of the technology perspective, which are the analysis target of the learning model, need to be defined. In addition to machine learning-related research, additional research needs to be considered in existing technology management fields. Next, it is necessary to review whether the lexical and syntactic features of technical information can be reflected in the input data of the model. For example, the existing NLP uses all words and dependency relations, but meaningful features could be reflected from a technical perspective, such as SAO structures in the encoding steps. In addition, building a learning model requires an annotated dataset for learning, which is considered the biggest obstacle.

In this paper, to overcome the limitations of previous studies, we develop a semantic relation model for technology information using F-term knowledge and deep learning models. The objectives of our study in this paper are as follows:

  • 1

    The types of semantic relations that are to be analysed are defined by delineating previous related studies, and these are dealt with in the background section. We focus on which semantic relations carry meaningful information in analysing technology.

  • 2

    Technological semantic relations are analysed using the F-term code, which is a multi-dimensional patent classification system. The F-term provides a hierarchical structure with a specific technological viewpoint. We use the F-term code system to provide knowledge of an entire range of technical fields in both hierarchical and textual information.

  • 3

    An annotated dataset of technological semantic relations has been secured by analysing the F-term in the previous step. A semantic relation extraction and classification model for technology information is suggested. We modify the existing RESIDE model based on the technological view (Vashishth, Joshi, Prayaga, Bhattacharyya, & Talukdar, 2018).

  • 4

    Finally, we develop a technology semantic relation for a target technology field by restoring the revised RESIDE model. The area of electric vehicles is chosen to illustrate their semantic relations.

The main contribution of this study is in developing a semantic relation model for technical documents by reflecting the characteristics of the technology that differ from general language. Moreover, a novel approach that evolved from the existing rule-based method to a neural learning model was developed. The rest of the paper is structured as follows: Section 2 delineates the background and reviews previous studies on semantic relations in textual technology information to define semantic relation types as well as F-term knowledge. Section 3 proposes the research framework to be used to develop a technological view of semantic relation and Section 4 conducts a case study in the electric automotive field to illustrate the proposed framework. Then, Section 5 discusses the results of the case study in detail. Finally, Section 6 concludes the paper by presenting the contribution of this research, its limitations, and opportunities for further study.

    • Development of a technology tree using patent information

      2024, Advanced Engineering Informatics

      A technology tree is used to represent technological attributes, such as the function, purpose, and components of a technology, and has been regarded as a valuable tool for investigating a technology in detail. However, most existing approaches to developing a technology tree considerably rely on experts; thus, the evaluation is subjective and does not reflect rapidly changing technological attributes under the paradigm of convergence. This study proposes the use of patent information to develop a robust technology tree. Subsequently, technological attributes were extracted in terms of key functions, and their relationships were analyzed to form a hierarchy. For the analysis, a series of methods were employed: clustering analysis to group patents into similar content, semantic analysis to identify key technological attributes in each group, and association-rule mining to define the relationships between the technological attributes. The proposed approach was then applied to a furniture manufacturing process to validate its applicability. Providing meaningful information on the technological structure will help improve the understanding of a target technology and further support research and development planning.

    • Deep purified feature mining model for joint named entity recognition and relation extraction

      2023, Information Processing and Management

      Table filling based joint named entity recognition and relation extraction task aims to share representation of subtasks in a table to extract structured knowledge. However, most of existing studies need additional labels and dedicated deep neural networks to learn shared representation, imposing heavy burdens to decoders. More seriously, almost all these models suffer from feature confusion problem, failing to capture purified task-specific features from shared representation to perform subtasks. To address these challenging problems, in this paper we propose a novel and effective Deep puRified fEAture Mining (DREAM) model for joint named entity recognition and relation extraction task, which can automatically capture purified task-specific features to improve the classification performance of subtasks. Specifically, unlike introducing additional labels or dedicated network architectures, we design a new lightweight shared representation learning (LSRL) module by the plainest labels of joint task and thus encodes context by the hybrid convolutional neural networks. Afterwards, a task-aware information bottleneck (TIB) module is proposed to explore the relation between the mutual information of the joint distribution of each subtask and its task-specific features. With the above two modules well obtain shared representation and purified task-specific features, the satisfactory classification results of both subtasks can be guaranteed. Experiment results show that the proposed model is highly effective, obtaining the promising results on three different benchmarks: CoNNL04 (general text), ADE (biomedical text) and SciERC (scientific text). For example, DREAM respectively achieves F1-scores of 78.18%, 80.28% and 44.60% in performing the relation extraction subtask on the CoNNL04, ADE and SciERC datasets. The promising performance indicates that the proposed model can be applied to many practical applications such as biomedical information extraction. The source code is publicly available at https://github.com/SWT-AITeam/DREAM.

    • ICAD-MI: Interdisciplinary concept association discovery from the perspective of metaphor interpretation

      2023, Knowledge-Based Systems

      Interdisciplinary concept association discovery is a fundamental task in interdisciplinary knowledge organization. Unlike general concept association, interdisciplinary concept association mainly manifests in the correlation between fine-grained concept properties, which requires that interdisciplinary concept association discovery be explored through a fine-grained semantic association discovery tool. Existing concept association discovery methods are limited in their ability to identify interdisciplinary concept associations at fine-grained conceptual properties because they can only identify which two concepts are associated at the coarse level. To bridge this gap, we propose a method we called interdisciplinary concept association discovery based on metaphor interpretation (ICAD-MI). First, we explored the mechanism of interdisciplinary conceptual metaphor on both the cognitive and language layers, which provides a solid foundation for our method. Second, we introduced the four-step ICAD-MI method, which integrates deep learning techniques with word semantics and multidimensional contexts. We tested the ICAD-MI framework using a dataset comprising a total of 1,915 data points of interdisciplinary metaphorical expressions (IMEs) on a typical interdisciplinary conceptual metaphor Computer is a brain. Our model achieved a precision of 94.4%, a recall of 73.9%, and an F1 score of 82.9%, which outperforms the four baseline methods. Additionally, we conducted parameter analysis to further validate the effectiveness of our proposed method. The code and datasets are publicly available at: https://github.com/haihua0913/ICADMI.
